Services

Services Offered by Our Office

Building Permit Renewal

This service allows property owners, developers, or authorized representatives to renew an existing building permit that is nearing expiration or has expired. The renewal ensures continued compliance with local construction regulations, safety standards, and zoning requirements. Applicants are required to submit the necessary documentation, pay applicable fees, and ensure that no significant changes have been made to the original approved plans. Renewing a building permit helps maintain legal authorization for ongoing or delayed construction activities..

Learn More

Demolition Permit

A Demolition Permit is an official authorization required before the partial or complete removal of any structure. This permit ensures that the demolition activity complies with safety regulations, environmental guidelines, and local zoning laws. Applicants must submit detailed information about the structure to be demolished, including its location, method of demolition, and plans for waste disposal or site restoration. The permit helps safeguard public safety and prevents damage to adjacent properties or infrastructure. .

Learn More

Night Construction Permit

A Nighttime Construction Permit grants authorization to carry out building or construction activities during nighttime hours, typically outside of regular working hours. This permit is required to ensure that such activities do not violate local noise ordinances, disturb nearby residents, or pose safety risks. Applicants must provide justification for night work, a detailed schedule, and mitigation measures for noise and light pollution. Approval is subject to review by relevant authorities to balance development needs with community well-being.

Learn More

Temporary Permit

A Temporary Permit is issued to authorize short-term use or activity that does not fall under standard or permanent regulations. This may include temporary structures, events, occupancy, signage, or construction-related activities. The permit ensures that all temporary operations comply with safety, zoning, and environmental standards for the specified period. Applicants must clearly define the nature, duration, and scope of the temporary activity and adhere to all conditions set by the issuing authority.

Learn More

Occupancy Permit

An Occupancy Permit is an official document that certifies a building or structure is safe and suitable for use or habitation. It is issued after inspections confirm that the construction complies with all relevant building codes, zoning regulations, and safety standards. This permit is typically required before a building can be legally occupied or used for its intended purpose.

Learn More

Information Provision Service

This service allows individuals, organizations, or stakeholders to access official information related to the office’s operations, regulations, procedures, and public services. It aims to promote transparency, accountability, and informed decision-making by providing accurate and timely information upon request or through designated communication channels.
